Security & Trust

How Cumulo Flight Deck protects your pilot logbook data. Honest, in plain English. No marketing.

Last updated: 2026-05-14

Plain-language promise
Your pilot logbook is some of the most sensitive professional data you have. It records your career, your medical, your hours, who you flew with. Cumulo treats it that way. This page explains exactly how — no marketing, no fluff. If you find something here that doesn't match what we actually do, email flycumulo@gmail.com and I'll fix it.

Where your data lives

Cumulo Flight Deck stores your flight data in two places:

  • On your device (browser localStorage, or future native app storage). This is the primary copy. The app works fully offline — you can add a flight in the cockpit between duties with no signal, and it persists locally.
  • In the cloud, on Supabase, in the ca-central-1 region — physically located in Montréal, Québec. This is the sync copy that lets you see your logbook on your phone, iPad, and laptop.

Both copies are yours. You can export everything to JSON or PDF at any time from Settings, with one click. You can delete everything from Settings → Privacy & Trust → "Delete + Purge", which wipes both copies within 30 days.

Why Montréal matters

Hosting in Canada means your flight data is governed by Canadian law (PIPEDA federally + Loi 25 in Québec) — not the US Patriot Act, FISA 702, or the CLOUD Act. For a Canadian pilot who flies into the US, this matters: your logbook records (including the names of crew members you flew with) cannot be compelled by US authorities through a US-based hosting provider.

Cumulo does not use AWS US-East, Azure US, or any US-based cloud for your primary data. Some auxiliary services (like AI OCR via Anthropic API) are US-hosted, but those are opt-in features and their use is explicitly disclosed in the Privacy Policy.

Who can access your data

Just you. Cumulo uses Supabase's Row Level Security (RLS) policies, which means each row in the database is tagged with your user_id and the database refuses queries that don't match. Even if someone breached Supabase's infrastructure, they could not read your data without your authenticated session.

Operationally:

  • The Cumulo founder has access to the Supabase admin dashboard, but RLS policies are enforced the same way for admin queries. There is no "magic admin bypass" — admin access is logged.
  • Supabase staff can technically access the underlying infrastructure (encrypted storage, encrypted backups). They have a Data Processing Agreement with Cumulo Inc. and operate under SOC 2 Type II controls.
  • Cloudflare proxies your HTTPS traffic (no decryption at the edge — Cumulo uses Full Strict TLS). They see metadata (IP, URL paths) for the duration of each request, then drop it.
  • Nobody else. Cumulo has no employees, no contractors, no support outsourcing. Just one Canadian pilot.

What we will and will NOT do with your data

What we DO
  • Store your flights, profile, and import audit log to make the app work.
  • Calculate aggregated, anonymous usage analytics (signup count, retention, churn) to improve the product.
  • Send transactional emails (password reset, billing, support replies).
  • Process AI OCR requests through Anthropic API only when you click "Import paper logbook from photo" — the image leaves your device only at that moment, and is not stored by Anthropic per their commercial terms.
  • Anonymize 3rd-party crew names (your captain or F/O on a given flight) when data leaves your device for cloud sync or shareable export — unless you've explicitly opted in to "I have crew consent" in your Profile.
What we DO NOT do
  • We will never sell your data. Not to airlines, not to insurance, not to advertisers, not to data brokers. Not even anonymized. This is a written commitment.
  • We will never serve advertising in Cumulo.
  • We will never use your flight data to train AI models.
  • We will never share your data with a third party, except: (a) Supabase + Cloudflare (necessary infra processors, contractually bound), (b) Stripe (when you pay — only billing info, not flight data), (c) law enforcement if we receive a valid Canadian warrant (which we will challenge, document publicly via a transparency report, and notify you of unless legally gagged).
  • We will never read your logbook entries for any reason other than fixing a bug you reported and only with your explicit per-incident consent.

Backup & recovery

  • Cloud: Supabase performs automatic daily backups, retained 7 days (free tier) or 30 days (Pro tier).
  • Your control: at any time, click Settings → "Export all my data" to download a complete JSON snapshot. We recommend doing this monthly. The JSON is human-readable and can be re-imported on any device or kept as a personal cold backup.
  • If our service is offline, your local copy keeps working. The app is offline-first. You can keep logging flights, exporting PDFs, and your data stays intact until sync comes back online.
  • If Cumulo shuts down (worst case), we commit to a 90-day notice period during which the export feature stays online, plus a public announcement and one final automated export emailed to every paying user.

Deletion policy

You can permanently delete your data at any time:

  • Local wipe: Settings → Privacy & Trust → "Delete + Purge". Instant. Reloads the app to a fresh-install state.
  • Cloud wipe: same action triggers a deletion request on Supabase. Your rows are marked for deletion immediately and physically purged within 30 days (this window exists for accidental-recovery, after which it's gone).
  • Trial expiry: if your 30-day trial ends without subscribing, your account locks but your data is preserved for 12 months in case you come back. After 12 months, the data is auto-purged with three warning emails (J-30, J-7, J-1).
  • Account closure: when you cancel, you keep access until the end of your billing period. After that, the trial-expiry policy above applies.

Captain & co-pilot names — the third-party privacy story

This is the most legally nuanced piece. Your roster imports often capture the names of other pilots you flew with (your captain when you're F/O, or the F/O when you're captain). Those people haven't consented to being in your Cumulo account. Cumulo handles this in four contexts:

  • On your device: full names are stored, exactly as you wrote in your paper logbook. Your personal logbook is a personal-use exception under PIPEDA s.4(2)(b) and Loi 25 art. 1 — Cumulo as the software vendor is not a "data controller" while data stays on your device.
  • TC PDF export (ramp check, ATPL submission): full names. PIPEDA s.7(3)(c.1)(i) explicitly permits regulatory disclosure, and TP 14052 expects identifiable PIC references.
  • Cloud sync: by default, third-party crew names are anonymized to initials (e.g. "J.S." for John Smith) when leaving your device. You can override via Profile → "Keep full crew names when syncing" — but only if you've actually obtained your crew's consent.
  • Shareable PDF export, JSON backup, share-with-anyone: same anonymization rule as cloud sync.

Your own name is never anonymized — it's not third-party data.

Incident response

If a security incident affects your data:

  • Within 72 hours of discovery, Cumulo will assess whether the incident meets the "real risk of significant harm" threshold (PIPEDA) or "risk of serious injury" threshold (Loi 25).
  • If yes: you will be emailed directly with: what happened, what data was affected, what we've done, and what you should do. The Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada and (for Québec users) the Commission d'accès à l'information will be notified per legal requirements.
  • Transparency report: every breach, every government data request, every subpoena will be logged in a public transparency report published annually on this page — except where a legal gag order prevents it.

Open-source roadmap

The Cumulo Flight Deck frontend is currently closed-source while the product is being commercially validated. There's a path to open-sourcing the client (TC compliance calculations, PDF export, parser code) in the future — at which point anyone can audit the trust claims on this page independently. This is a "when the business is sustainable, not before" decision. Let me know if open-source matters to you.

How to verify these claims

  1. Network inspection: open browser DevTools → Network. Every request is HTTPS. The only third-party hosts called are: cdn.jsdelivr.net (libraries Chart.js / jsPDF / pdf.js / Supabase SDK), fonts.googleapis.com + fonts.gstatic.com (Inter font), logbook-api.martindaoust33.workers.dev (Cloudflare Worker proxy for Anthropic API + Navblue iCal CORS), and your Supabase project URL.
  2. Subprocessors: full list in the Privacy Policy §5.
  3. Export your data: Settings → Export. The JSON shows exactly what's stored. No hidden fields.
  4. Audit log: every import operation writes a timestamped entry to localStorage key cumulo_import_log_v1. Read it yourself.
